## 📖 Free Sample: Part 2 (The STAR Framework)

Google interviewers evaluate behavioral answers strictly against the **STAR (Situation, Task, Action, Result)** framework. If your answer does not cover all four components, the interviewer's scorecard will have blanks, which correlates with a low score.

### 🌟 STAR Breakdown for Google:
1. **Situation (10-15%)**: Set the scene. One to three sentences. Give enough context to understand the stakes, but do not narrate.
2. **Task (10-15%)**: Detail your specific responsibility. What were YOU accountable for? Avoid blurring your personal ownership with team goals.
3. **Action (55-65%)**: The core of your answer. Walk through the concrete steps YOU took: what you decided, who you influenced, what you said, and what you chose *not* to do and why.
4. **Result (15-20%)**: Quantified impact. Numbers are credibility. Mention metrics, timelines, scale, and long-term learnings.

---

### 🚨 The 4 STAR Anti-patterns Google Watches For:

#### 1. The Team Story (Using "We" instead of "I")
* **❌ BAD**: *"Our team migrated the database in 3 months and we reduced API latency by 40%."*
* **✅ GOOD**: *"I designed the migration plan and wrote the design doc. I ran 3 design reviews, resolved a conflict between the infra team and product over consistency trade-offs, and drove the execution."*
* **💡 Why**: Google hires individuals, not groups. They need to score your specific contributions.

#### 2. The Shallow Result
* **❌ BAD**: *"The launch was successful and the team was happy with the outcome."*
* **✅ GOOD**: *"Launched to 100% of users in 6 weeks. P99 latency dropped from 2.1s to 180ms. On-call pages decreased by 70% in the first month."*
* **💡 Why**: Hiring committees cannot verify vague results. Numbers build trust.

#### 3. The Passive Story
* **❌ BAD**: *"I was asked to migrate the legacy system, so I followed the instructions and coordinated the work."*
* **✅ GOOD**: *"I proactively identified that our legacy auth system was a major timeline risk. I built the business case, got buy-in from the PM, proposed a migration plan, and led it to completion."*
* **💡 Why**: Google wants self-starters who drive outcomes, not just task executors.

#### 4. No Conflict or Failure
* **❌ BAD**: Choosing easy stories where everything went perfectly and everyone agreed.
* **✅ GOOD**: Choosing stories with genuine tension — disagreements, architectural trade-offs, or failures.
* **💡 Why**: Google values intellectual humility and growth. Showing how you navigated a mistake or disagreement is a high-score signal.
